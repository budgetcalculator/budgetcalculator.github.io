Know Before You Buy

Buying a home is the biggest financial decision of your life. Don't rely on the "estimated payment" on listing sitesâ€”they often leave out critical costs like taxes and insurance. Our advanced mortgage calculator gives you the full picture.

Pairing this with a monthly budget planner is essential. Your house payment shouldn't just fit your bank's criteria; it needs to fit your actual life.

How to Use This Mortgage Tool

Accuracy matters when thousands of dollars are on the line. Here is how to use this online mortgage planner:

  • Down Payment: Aim for 20% to avoid Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), but many loans allow as little as 3%.
  • Interest Rate: Check current market averages for your credit score to get an accurate estimate.
  • Property Tax: Look up the local tax rate for the area you are shopping in.
  • Home Insurance: Get a quote or use the national average of around $1,500/year as a placeholder.

Fitting a Home into Your Budget

A common rule is that your "PITI" (Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance) should not exceed 28% of your gross income. If you use our 50 30 20 rule calculator, your mortgage falls into the 50% "Needs" category.

By using this tool alongside an income tax calculator, you can determine exactly how much house you can afford based on your actual take-home pay, not just your salary.

The Impact of Extra Payments

Just like our debt payoff calculator shows, small extra payments on your mortgage principal can save you tens of thousands in interest over 30 years. Even an extra $100 a month can shave years off your loan term.
